Locations In Game Of Thrones

Most of the story takes place on the continent of Westeros and in a large political entity known as the Seven Kingdoms. Those kingdoms are spread across nine regions: the North, the Iron Islands, the Riverlands, the Vale, the Westerlands, the Stormlands, the Reach, the Crownlands, and Dorne.

1.Winterfell

The ancestral seat of House Stark, located in the North of Westeros. Winterfell serves as one of the primary settings for much of the series, known for its imposing castle and vast surrounding lands.

2.King's Landing:

The capital city of the Seven Kingdoms, located on the eastern coast of Westeros. King's Landing is known for its Red Keep, where the Iron Throne is housed, and its bustling streets filled with intrigue and political machinations.

3.The Wall:

A massive ice structure located in the far North of Westeros, separating the Seven Kingdoms from the lands beyond. Manned by the Night's Watch, the Wall is meant to protect the realm from threats such as the White Walkers.

4. Dragonstone:

A volcanic island fortress located off the coast of Westeros. Dragonstone was the ancestral seat of House Targaryen and served as their stronghold before their conquest of the Seven Kingdoms. It is known for its imposing castle and its history with dragons.

5. The Eyrie:

A castle located high in the Mountains of the Moon, ruled by House Arryn. The Eyrie is known for its impregnability and its unique architecture, as well as the eerie Moon Door, through which prisoners are executed by being thrown out into the sky.
